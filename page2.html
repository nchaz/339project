MONTREAL CANADIENS PLAYOFF RUN

ROUND 2


Winnipeg's Top 6
Youth Movement
Hellebuyck vs. Price
There aren't many holes in Winnipeg's lineup aside from their defense. With a strong top 6 presence, Winnipeg looks to disrupt Montreal's momentum early and show why their offense is one of the best in the league.
With big games and big goals by Kotkaniemi and Suzuki in round 1, the Habs look to ride their momentum into their series with Winnipeg, a team who looked like they could be the favorite out of the North division after Toronto's collapse.
With arguably two of the leagues best goalies going head to head in as early as the second round, it should be a battle of the goalies between the Habs and the Jets in what may come down to whichever team's goalie plays better advances.

GAME 1


MONTREAL WINS 5-3

MONTREAL LEADS 1-0

With Montreal dominating game 1 to steal a win from the Jets at home, this doesn't come without a bad taste in the mouths of Habs fans when Scheifele throws a suspendable hit on Evans in the last minutes of the game.

GAME 2


MONTREAL WINS 1-0

MONTREAL LEADS 2-0

In a very defense and goalie centric game on home ice for Winnipeg, they simply can't break Carey Price, who goes on to steal game 2 for Montreal with a hardfought 1-0 win.

GAME 3


MONTREAL WINS 5-1

MONTREAL LEADS 3-0

Montreal comes out firing to take both a commanding win over the Jets of 5-1 at home, and a commanding series lead at 3-0, making this series all but done for Winnipeg.

GAME 4


MONTREAL WINS 3-2 (OT)

MONTREAL ADVANCES 4-0

In an action packed final game of the series, Toffoli nets home a cross-crease goal assisted by Caufield to send Montreal to the Conference Finals for the first time since 2014!
