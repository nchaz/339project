MONTREAL CANADIENS PLAYOFF RUN


CONFERENCE FINALS

Rest vs. 7 Games
Toffoli/Suzuki/Caufield
Pacioretty vs. Suzuki
In an age old debate, who comes away as having the better situation? The team that swept their last opponent and hasn't played in a week, or the one who hopes to keep their momentum going after a hardfought 7 game series.
With this line firing on all cylinders in the second round against the Winnipeg Jets, Vegas's defensemen will be put to the test when Cole Caufield, Tyler Toffoli, and Nick Suzuki come out on the ice.
With the two being traded for one another, Max Pacioretty and Nick Suzuki finally go head to head in a playoff matchup that relies on much more than just these two players, yet still is a storyline going into the series.

GAME 1


VEGAS WINS 4-1

VEGAS LEADS 1-0

Vegas ends Montreal's win streak at 7 games after taking a commanding win at home against Montreal. Hockey fans rejoice as they know that Montreal can't keep up with a team like Vegas, and it showed tonight.

GAME 2


MONTREAL WINS 3-2

SERIES TIED 1-1

Montreal steals a game on the road from Vegas with a well-battled 3-2 win over Marc-Andre Fleury and the Golden Knights.

GAME 3


MONTREAL WINS 3-2 (OT)

MONTREAL LEADS 2-1

After a gaffe that sees Fleury hand Josh Anderson a goal at the end of regulation to force overtime, Montreal comes out with a vegenance to see none other than Anderson again be the one to seal the deal to take a 2-1 series lead.

GAME 4


VEGAS WINS 2-1 (OT)

SERIES TIED 2-2

This series finds itself once again in an overtime battle, this time with Quebec-native Nic Roy putting Vegas out on top, to tie this series at 2-2. Montreal fans worry as winning this game felt like one of their last hopes.

GAME 5


MONTREAL WINS 4-1

MONTREAL LEADS 3-2

Montreal comes out hot and does not relent for the remainder of the game. They take another game on the road that sees Vegas be put on the brink of elimination.

GAME 6


MONTREAL WINS 3-2 (OT)

MONTREAL ADVANCES 4-2

"Danault, passes to Lehkonen, who shoots, and he scores!" Just like that, Montreal wins and for the first time sine 1993, seems themselves in the final round where they will face Tampa Bay to fight for the Stanley Cup.
